Recommendation
Where to go from here
These three directions represent a spectrum from industrial precision to bold statement to refined authority. They are not mutually exclusive. The final system will likely combine elements. Here is how they compare.
A / The Engineer B / The Statement C / The Architect
Character Industrial. Functional. German heritage. Bold. Compressed. Cultural impact. Contemporary. Clean. Global tech.
Closest to DIN / Porsche / Braun Bureau Borsche / ACG / ACRONYM Apple / Aesop / Arc'teryx
Wordmark feel Wide, stable, engineering-grade Tall, compressed, maximum presence Geometric, balanced, premium
Blueprint layer Strongest. IBM Plex Mono has technical DNA. Good. Space Mono has character. Clean. JetBrains Mono is precise.
Scalability Excellent. Full weight range. Limited. Bebas is display-only. Best. Outfit covers all use cases.
Risk Could feel too industrial / cold. Could feel too loud / trend-driven. Could feel too safe / generic.
Open source? Yes (both fonts) Yes (both fonts) Yes (both fonts)
License upgrade DIN 2014 + FF DIN Mono Druk + GT America Mono GT America + Söhne Mono
Initial take
Direction B with the data layer of A might be the strongest combination. The bold condensed wordmark creates the stadium presence and manifesto energy the brand foundation calls for. The IBM Plex Mono technical layer provides the blueprint credibility. And the commercial upgrade path (Druk + FF DIN Mono) would be world-class.

But this is your call. Look at how "ALVEON" feels in each direction. Which one would you want to see on a boot? On a building? On a scan report?
